## What I Studied and What I Can Do

My MSc in Artificial Intelligence trained me to design and build intelligent systems from end to end. This included framing real-world problems in a responsible way, considering ethics and risk, validating claims with statistics, building machine learning models for prediction and pattern discovery, creating text-based features, representing knowledge through ontologies for explainable reasoning, and developing intelligent agents that can act securely while maintaining an auditable record of their behaviour.

## Dissertation

My dissertation is included in the **MSc Computing Project** section of this repository. It focuses on the development of an **AI-powered early-warning framework** for the early detection and mitigation of delays and cost overruns in construction project management.

The study is built around project-controls data and a transparent dashboard-based approach. It combines **Earned Value Management (EVM) indicators**, **interpretable scoring methods**, and **comparative AI models** to support practical and explainable decision-making.

## Technical Toolkit & Applied Platforms

My academic work involved a mix of machine learning, statistics, knowledge modelling, dashboards, and secure intelligent systems. Below is a summary of the main tools and platforms I used, together with the kind of work they supported.

### Machine Learning and AI Development
- **Python**
- **scikit-learn**
- **TensorFlow / Keras**
- **LightGBM**
- **WEKA**

I used these tools to build and compare machine learning models, test different approaches, create text features, and evaluate results across classification, regression, clustering, and experimental AI workflows. Python formed the main programming environment, while scikit-learn supported structured pipelines and evaluation. TensorFlow and Keras supported deeper learning workflows, LightGBM provided strong tabular modelling baselines, and WEKA was useful for rapid comparison and explainable experimentation.

### Data Preparation and Numerical Analysis
- **Pandas**
- **NumPy**
- **R**
- **dplyr**

These tools supported data cleaning, reshaping, validation, descriptive analysis, and statistical preparation. They were central to working with raw datasets, preparing them for modelling, and making sure analysis was based on clean and reliable inputs.

### Statistics, Visualisation, and Reporting
- **Matplotlib**
- **Seaborn**
- **ggplot2**
- **Advanced Excel**

I used these tools for exploratory data analysis, statistical visualisation, result reporting, and clear presentation of findings. They helped turn technical outputs into readable charts, trends, and comparisons that could support discussion and decision-making.

### Dashboards and Interactive Applications
- **Dash / Plotly**
- **R Shiny**
- **Power BI**
- **Jupyter Notebooks**

These were used to communicate results in a more interactive and accessible way. Dash and Shiny supported dashboard-style outputs, Power BI supported reporting and visual summaries, and Jupyter Notebooks were used to combine code, analysis, and explanation in a structured and reproducible format.

### Knowledge Representation and Reasoning
- **Protégé**
- **OWL**
- **UML**

These tools supported ontology development, rule-based knowledge representation, and structured system thinking. They were especially useful where explainability and formal reasoning were important.

### Databases, Security, and Auditability
- **SQL**
- **SQLite**
- **Paramiko**
- **SHA-256 hashing**

These tools supported structured data storage, query-based access, secure file transfer, and integrity verification. They were particularly useful in intelligent-agent work where traceability, secure handling, and auditability mattered.

### Workflow, Version Control, and Collaboration
- **Git**
- **GitHub**

These supported version control, structured project organisation, documentation, and reproducible development across different parts of the MSc portfolio.

### Project Controls and Scheduling Tools
- **Primavera P6 (Expert)**
- **Microsoft Project**

These tools sit alongside my wider technical toolkit and reflect my background in project controls and scheduling. They support planning, baseline management, logic review, progress tracking, and schedule analysis.
